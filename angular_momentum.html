Conservation of Angular Momentum

(Rotational Motion)

Academic Information

Class: 11

Book Name: Physics – I

Chapter No: 6

Chapter: System of Particles and Rotational Motion

Learning Objectives

  • Recognize that in a closed system (no external torques), the total angular momentum remains constant.
  • Identify how angular momentum plays a role in situations such as gyroscopes, ice skaters spinning faster by pulling in their arms, or the stability of planets in their orbits.

Science Involved

  • If no external torque acts on a body in motion, angular momentum (moment of inertia × angular velocity) remains constant.
  • When pulling weights towards the body, the moment of inertia decreases, and speed increases, conserving angular momentum.

Participate & Observe

  • Sit on the turntable with weights on both sides. Slide weights to the farthest point and have a friend slowly set the turntable in motion.
  • Slide the weights towards your body to increase speed and push them back to decrease speed.
  • Hold a cycle wheel straight at a right angle and tilt it left or right to rotate the turntable accordingly, demonstrating the gyroscope principle.

Applications

  • Stability: Angular momentum is crucial for the stability of spinning objects like tops and gyroscopes.
  • Astrophysics: Understanding the rotation of planets, stars, and galaxies.
  • Quantum Mechanics: Describing the behavior of subatomic particles and intrinsic spin.
